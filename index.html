Crime & Detective

Lies, secrets, twists—truth is a puzzle.

A thinking person’s world. Detectives, informants, hidden clues, false trails.
Every clue must be placed with purpose. Ideal for branching plots and multiple endings.

🧩 What Makes This World Type Unique?

  • Truth is buried under layers of lies, fear, and manipulation.
  • The protagonist is often more flawed than heroic.
  • Settings tend to be grounded—urban, gritty, or noir-styled.
  • Time and sequence matter: what you learn and when changes everything.

🔍 Worldbuilding Tips

  • Build a case file: who’s involved, what happened, what’s being hidden.
  • Design each location with evidence or red herrings in mind.
  • Use dialogue trees or written journals to hide and reveal facts.
  • Establish moral ambiguity—what if the truth causes more harm?

🧠 Narrative Ideas

  • A cold case reopens with new evidence that implicates the detective.
  • A child’s disappearance uncovers decades of town-wide corruption.
  • An anonymous tip forces a retired detective back into their worst case.
  • A murder mystery on a train—everyone has a motive, but no one has an alibi.

🎮 Game Design Possibilities

  • Clue-tracking system with branching suspect logic trees.
  • Evidence boards players can physically arrange.
  • Dialogue puzzles where phrasing affects outcomes.
  • Multiple endings depending on who is accused—and whether they’re guilty.
Detective stories reward curiosity. Let the player become the one who sees what others missed.
